*Written for the Vancouver Sun*

Opinion: Promises by politicians to “scrap the tax” need to provide policy 
proposals for what they will do instead. Doing nothing commits us to higher 
costs and a more unstable climate for our children.
------
On Friday, Premier David Eby unexpectedly reversed the government’s position on 
carbon pricing. The premier said that if Ottawa removes its consumer carbon tax
requirement, B.C. would repeal ours.

This reversal, alongside a similar shift by the federal NDP, vociferous 
opposition from the federal Tories, and outright climate skepticism from the 
Conservative Party of B.C., suggests that the days of consumer carbon pricing 
may be numbered.

But before we throw away a longstanding and demonstrably effective tool to 
reduce emissions, we need to have a frank conversation about costs.
