*Written with Katya Rhodes (University of Victoria) for The Conversation*

Better coordination of climate policies across federal, provincial and 
territorial governments is essential to achieving Canada's emissions targets.
------
Governments across Canada have implemented a myriad of policies to reduce 
greenhouse gas emissions in recent years, layered across federal, provincial 
and territorial levels. While overlapping policies can reinforce one another, 
misaligned approaches risk unintended consequences that undermine climate goals.

Drawing on our research examining 184 climate policies and 356 policy 
interactions across Canada's federal government and its four most populous 
provinces, we argue that deliberately synchronizing policy mixes across 
jurisdictional levels — rather than layering policies ad hoc — is key to 
reducing emissions efficiently.
