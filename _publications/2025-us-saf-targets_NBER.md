Decarbonizing aviation is expected to rely heavily on sustainable aviation fuels (SAFs), primarily biofuels. This paper develops a partial equilibrium model of road and air transportation fuel markets to evaluate policy approaches for achieving the US target of 3 billion gallons of SAF by 2030. We find that the target is attainable with current technology but not with current policy. Alternative federal policy options — including modifications to the Renewable Fuel Standard, federal SAF tax credits, and clean aviation standards — could meet the target with comparable emissions reductions and costs but different distributional effects, and SAF policies necessarily interact with existing surface transportation biofuel policies at the federal and state levels.
