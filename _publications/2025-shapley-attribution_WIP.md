When multiple climate policies overlap, attributing observed emission reductions to individual policies is conceptually and empirically challenging. This work in progress applies a Shapley value approach from cooperative game theory to fairly allocate emission reductions among overlapping policy instruments.

Presented at the Canadian Resource and Environmental Economics Association (CREEA) Annual Conference, Winnipeg, MB, October 17, 2025.
